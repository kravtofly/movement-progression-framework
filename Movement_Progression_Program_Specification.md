# Movement Progression Program Specification

## Version 1.0: Master Specification

### Status

Authoritative v1.0 Release

### Purpose

This document serves as the authoritative source of truth for the Movement Progression Program.

It defines the philosophy, structure, standards, governance, evaluation model, and progression architecture that underpin the program.

All future program materials, training resources, evaluation systems, software tools, review surfaces, and AI systems should derive from this specification.

This document is intended to preserve not only standards, but also the reasoning and design decisions that produced those standards.

This v1.0 release integrates the complete Flyer and Leader Tiers (Solo through Lead 4), the Evaluator level, the constitutional Implementation Discoveries identified during development, and the governance safeguards required to maintain the framework over time.

# Part I: Constitutional Layer

# Program Mission

To develop safe, competent movement flyers through a structured progression system that emphasizes responsibility, competency, judgment, continuous learning, and stewardship of standards.

The program exists to:

- Develop safe movement participants.
- Develop safe movement jump leaders.
- Develop competent evaluators.
- Preserve institutional knowledge.
- Maintain standards across generations of flyers.
- Reduce movement-related risk throughout the movement community.

# Foundational Philosophy

## FP-001: Competency-Based Progression

Advancement is based on demonstrated competency rather than accumulated experience.

Jump numbers, licenses, reputation, popularity, confidence, and perceived talent may provide context but are not primary indicators of readiness.

Competency must be demonstrated.

## FP-002: Responsibility-Based Progression

Progression represents increasing responsibility.

Skills are evidence used to demonstrate readiness for responsibility.

Progression is not primarily the accumulation of privileges.

Progression is acceptance of responsibility.

## FP-003: Operational Certifications and Developmental Ladder

The progression system serves two purposes simultaneously.

Operationally:

The stickers communicate capability, authority, and operational privileges.

Developmentally:

The stickers represent stages of growth, judgment, responsibility, and stewardship.

The program is therefore:

- A certification ladder on the surface.
- A developmental ladder underneath.

## FP-004: Responsibility Precedes Privilege

Privileges are earned only after demonstrating readiness to accept the responsibilities associated with those privileges.

Responsibility is the organizing principle of progression.

## FP-005: Standards Protect People

Standards exist to:

- Protect participants.
- Promote safety.
- Preserve consistency.

Standards do not exist to create hierarchy, exclusivity, status, or barriers.

The value of a standard should be measured by its contribution to safety, consistency, and development.

## FP-006: Demonstrated Competency Matters More Than Potential

Candidates are evaluated based on demonstrated competency.

Evaluators do not certify:

- Potential
- Talent
- Optimism
- Future capability

Evaluators certify demonstrated competency.

# Program Culture

The following expectations apply to all participants.

These expectations are cultural values rather than pass/fail evaluation criteria.

Participants are expected to:

- Approach movement with humility.
- Remain open to learning.
- Accept coaching professionally.
- Prioritize safety over performance.
- Seek understanding before advancement.
- Contribute positively to the learning environment.
- Recognize that earning a sticker marks the beginning of a new stage of development.

## Teachability

Teachability is a cultural value.

Teachability is not an evaluation criterion.

### Rationale

Teachability is essential to growth but difficult to evaluate objectively and consistently.

Treating teachability as a pass/fail criterion creates unnecessary subjectivity.

The program encourages teachability through culture rather than enforcing it through evaluation checklists.

# Program Principles

## PP-001: Movement Requires Intentional Planning

Movement flight is fundamentally different from static skydiving.

Movement introduces:

- Heading selection
- Separation management
- Flight planning
- Deployment planning
- Collision risk management

These risks are often invisible to inexperienced participants.

Movement-specific training and evaluation are therefore required.


## PP-005: Institutional Knowledge Must Be Preserved

The program should preserve:

- Coaching knowledge
- Evaluation knowledge
- Safety knowledge
- Lessons learned

Critical knowledge should not exist solely in the minds of experienced coaches, evaluators, or examiners.

## PP-006: Rationale Is As Important As Requirements

Requirements alone are insufficient.

The program should preserve:

- What the standard is.
- Why the standard exists.
- How the standard should be evaluated.

Future maintainers should be able to understand the intent behind standards.

# Implementation Discoveries

The following constitutional principles were not part of the original design. They were discovered and verified during the implementation of the Flyer and Leader Tiers. They are recorded here to distinguish between what the framework was designed to be and what the implementation proved it to be.

## ID-001: Dual Development Principle

The Movement Progression Framework intentionally develops two parallel competencies:

- Technical movement competency
- Leadership judgment

Neither technical ability nor judgment alone is sufficient for advancement.

The framework deliberately develops both simultaneously through progressively increasing responsibility.

## ID-002: Progressive Responsibility Principle

Responsibility is the primary developmental variable throughout the framework.

Advancement reflects the demonstrated ability to responsibly exercise the privileges associated with each level rather than merely demonstrating technical proficiency.

## ID-003: Developmental Guardrails Principle

Operational restrictions exist to serve both safety and development.

Restrictions intentionally narrow the learning environment to reduce unnecessary complexity while specific competencies are developed.

Operational restrictions are progressively removed only after the associated knowledge, judgment, and responsibility have been demonstrated.

## ID-004: Operational Freedom Principle

Operational freedom increases only as demonstrated judgment becomes increasingly reliable.

The framework intentionally transfers responsibility for operational decision-making from the specification to the individual as leadership competency matures.

## ID-005: Judgment Principle

The ultimate objective of the Leadership Tier is not unrestricted operational freedom.

The objective is the demonstrated ability to consistently exercise sound judgment when determining what is appropriate for the participants, conditions, and objectives of each movement jump.

## ID-006: Leadership Stewardship Principle

Leadership within the Movement Progression Framework is an act of stewardship.

The purpose of leadership is to create movement jumps that are safe, enjoyable, appropriately challenging, and developmentally valuable for every participant.

Leadership exists to serve the participants rather than the leader.

## ID-007: Purposeful Complexity Principle

Complexity is introduced only when it serves a clear developmental purpose.

The framework intentionally treats complexity as a tool for learning, progression, and leadership development rather than as an objective in itself.

## ID-008: Stewardship Over Status Principle

Advancement within the Movement Progression Framework recognizes increasing responsibility, demonstrated competency, and stewardship rather than personal status or technical accomplishment alone.

The purpose of progression is to improve the quality, safety, and developmental value of movement jumps for everyone involved.

## ID-009: Evidence-Based Advancement Principle

Advancement within the Movement Progression Framework is based upon demonstrated competency supported by sufficient evidence rather than assumptions, reputation, jump numbers, or self-assessment.

The framework intentionally favors observable performance over perceived ability.

## Framework Mission Statement

The framework exists to systematically develop movement flyers into increasingly capable leaders through progressively increasing responsibility, sound judgment, and demonstrated competency.

Every standard, requirement, restriction, and privilege within this specification serves that mission.

# Responsibility Model

Every level represents an increase in responsibility.

| Level | Primary Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| Solo | Manage yourself |
| Group | Manage your position within a group |
| Lead 1 | Manage one other flyer |
| Lead 2 | Manage a formation |
| Lead 3 | Introduce and manage complexity appropriately |
| Lead 4 | Exercise sound judgment while stewarding the entire movement jump experience |
| Evaluator | Develop people while protecting standards |
| Examiner | Develop evaluators and protect standards |

This responsibility ladder should guide future level design.

# Responsibility Transition Model

A recurring pattern exists throughout the progression system.

| Transition | Responsibility Accepted |
| --- | --- |
| Non-Solo → Solo | Responsibility for planning and conducting safe personal movement jumps |
| Solo → Group | Responsibility for safe participation within a movement group |
| Group → Leader Tier | Responsibility for the safety and management of other flyers |
| Leader Tier → EE Tier | Responsibility for the progression and development of other flyers and the protection of standards |

This model should guide future sticker design.

### Status

Accepted

### Rationale

Jump numbers are an unreliable indicator of competence.

Two individuals with identical jump numbers may possess dramatically different levels of knowledge, judgment, skill, and risk awareness.

Advancement should therefore be based on demonstrated competency rather than accumulated experience.

### Implications

- Jump numbers are not primary advancement criteria.
- Progression speed may vary significantly between individuals.
- Evaluators play a critical role in advancement decisions.
- Demonstrated competency always takes precedence over accumulated experience.

Accepted

# Part II: Specification Architecture

## Purpose

This section defines the structural architecture used throughout the Movement Progression Program.

The purpose of this architecture is to:

- Promote consistency.
- Preserve institutional knowledge.
- Improve evaluation quality.
- Simplify future expansion.
- Support software implementation.
- Support AI-assisted review and education.

All future levels shall conform to the structures defined in this section.

# Level Architecture

Every level within the progression system shall contain the following sections.

## 1. Intent Statement

The Intent Statement explains:

- Why the level exists.
- What developmental stage it represents.
- What transformation is expected of the candidate.

Intent Statements communicate developmental purpose.

## 2. Responsibility

Responsibility defines the primary responsibility accepted by a candidate at that level.

Responsibility is the organizing principle of progression.

All other sections should support the accepted responsibility.

## 3. Prerequisites

Prerequisites define minimum entry requirements.

Examples may include:

- Licenses
- Certifications
- Experience requirements
- Courses
- Recommendations

Prerequisites define eligibility.

They do not guarantee readiness.

## 4. Knowledge

Knowledge requirements define concepts the candidate must understand.

Knowledge may be evaluated through:

- Discussion
- Questioning
- Scenario analysis
- Observation of decision making

Knowledge requirements should focus on understanding rather than memorization.

## 5. Skills

Skills define observable competencies.

Skills should be:

- Observable
- Demonstrable
- Evaluatable

Every skill should have a unique identifier.

## 6. Evaluation Criteria

Evaluation Criteria define the conditions under which certification may be granted.

Evaluation criteria answer:

What evidence is required to determine competency?

## 7. Privileges

Privileges define what becomes authorized after certification.

Privileges are not requirements.

Privileges are operational capabilities granted by certification.

## 8. Restrictions

Restrictions define operational boundaries.

Restrictions communicate:

- What remains unauthorized.
- What requires future progression.
- What risks remain outside the candidate’s authority.

## 9. Advancement Path

Advancement Path defines the next available progression options.

The purpose is to provide developmental continuity.

## 10. Duplication Audit

Every level shall include a duplication audit.

The duplication audit identifies:

- New competencies
- Reinforced competencies
- Expanded competencies
- Consolidation candidates

The goal is to maintain progression clarity.

# Requirement Architecture

Every requirement shall contain three layers.

## Layer 1: Requirement

The requirement defines the observable competency.

The requirement should be concise, objective, and evaluatable.

Example:

Determine the correct heading while planning the jump.

## Layer 2: Student Explanation

The Student Explanation answers:

- Why does this requirement exist?
- Why does it matter?
- What problem does it solve?

The purpose is education.

Student explanations preserve learning value.

## Layer 3: Evaluator Guidance

Evaluator Guidance answers:

- What should be observed?
- What constitutes competency?
- What warning signs should be recognized?
- What common mistakes occur?

The purpose is consistency.

Evaluator guidance preserves institutional knowledge.

# Skill Formatting Standard

Skills are formatted identically across all levels so that specification content can be parsed reliably by software.

The canonical structure is:

- `## Skills`: the skills section of a level.
- `### Category Name`: an optional grouping heading. A category heading never matches the skill identifier pattern.
- `#### LEVEL-DOM-NNN`: a skill identifier. Skill identifiers always appear at heading level four and always match the pattern `PREFIX-DOMAIN-NUMBER` (for example, `LEAD2-FRM-001`).
- The requirement statement appears as the first paragraph following the skill identifier.
- `##### Student Explanation`: Layer 2 of the requirement.
- `##### Evaluator Guidance`: Layer 3 of the requirement.
- `Duplication Audit: <classification>`: a single plain-text line at the end of the skill recording the requirement's duplication classification (for example, `Duplication Audit: 🟢 New`).

Parsing rules:

- A skill is identified exclusively by its heading pattern, never by position.
- Everything between one skill identifier heading and the next heading of level four or higher belongs to that skill.
- Categories are optional. Skills may appear directly beneath the Skills section.

This standard is normative. Derivative software should reject content that deviates from it.

# Evaluation Architecture

The purpose of evaluation is to determine whether a candidate has demonstrated competency sufficient to safely exercise the privileges associated with a level.

Evaluation is not intended to:

- Reward effort.
- Reward participation.
- Reward loyalty.
- Reward potential.

Evaluation exists to certify competency.

# Evaluation Outcomes

The program recognizes two outcomes.

## Ready

The candidate has demonstrated competency.

Certification may be granted.

## Not Yet

The candidate has not yet demonstrated competency.

Additional development is required.

“Not Yet” is a developmental outcome.

It is not a rejection.

# Competency Framework

Evaluators should distinguish between three levels of performance.

## Completion

The candidate performed the task.

## Competency

The candidate performed the task:

- Safely
- Correctly
- Consistently
- With control

## Ownership

The candidate demonstrates:

- Comfort
- Consistency
- Intentionality
- Normalized behavior

Ownership indicates that competency has become habitual.

# Evidence Architecture

Certification decisions should be evidence-based.

Evidence may include:

- Direct observation
- Video review
- Structured discussion
- Scenario evaluation

Evidence should be observable and defensible.

# Video Philosophy

Video is the primary evidence artifact throughout the program.

Video allows:

- Review
- Feedback
- Consistency
- Retrospective analysis

Video should be used whenever practical.

Video does not replace evaluator judgment.

Video supports evaluator judgment.

# Sign-Off Architecture

A sign-off represents a professional endorsement.

The evaluator is effectively stating:

I am willing to personally stand behind the statement that this individual can safely exercise the privileges associated with this certification.

Sign-offs should never be treated as administrative actions.

Sign-offs are professional certifications of demonstrated competency.

# Standards Preservation Principle

The purpose of the progression system is not merely to produce certifications.

The purpose is to produce competent participants while preserving the standards that make those certifications meaningful.

Every architectural decision should be evaluated against this objective.

# Leader Tier

## Purpose

Develop safe, capable movement jump leaders through progressively increasing responsibility.

## Leadership Defined

Leadership within the Movement Progression Framework is the responsible application of movement knowledge, judgment, communication, and decision-making in service of other participants.

Leadership is distinct from flying ability. A technically excellent flyer is not necessarily a capable leader.

## Tier Architecture

The Leadership Tier intentionally develops increasingly capable movement leaders by progressively increasing responsibility while gradually transferring operational decision-making from the framework to the individual leader.

The progression follows a single developmental sequence:

1. Responsibility increases at every level.
2. Judgment develops through the repeated exercise of that responsibility.
3. Operational freedom expands only as demonstrated judgment becomes increasingly reliable.
4. Complexity is introduced only when it serves that development.

Each level answers a distinct developmental question:

| Level | Central Question |
| --- | --- |
| Lead 1 | Can I lead? |
| Lead 2 | Can I lead consistently? |
| Lead 3 | Can I introduce complexity appropriately? |
| Lead 4 | Can I consistently exercise sound judgment without external guardrails? |

## Developmental Guardrails

The operational restrictions within the Leadership Tier are developmental tools, not merely safety limits.

Each restriction intentionally narrows the learning environment while a specific leadership competency is developed. Restrictions are progressively removed as the associated knowledge, judgment, and responsibility are demonstrated, until Lead 4, where the framework transfers operational decision-making entirely to the leader.

A restriction should never be removed from this tier without identifying the developmental objective that would disappear with it.

## Tier Purpose Statement

The Leadership Tier does not exist to produce increasingly advanced movement flyers.

It exists to develop leaders capable of consistently exercising sound judgment while creating safe, enjoyable, and developmentally valuable movement jumps.

# Lead 4

Operationally Complete

## Development Theme

Demonstrated Discernment

## Intent Statement

Lead 4 represents the culmination of the Leadership Tier.

A Lead 4 flyer has demonstrated the judgment, maturity, and leadership necessary to safely plan, organize, lead, and debrief movement jumps without relying on externally imposed operational guardrails.

Rather than expanding a leader's technical toolbox, Lead 4 demonstrates that the leader has internalized the principles, responsibilities, and judgment developed throughout the entire Leadership Tier.

A Lead 4 flyer understands how to design movement jumps appropriate for the participants, the conditions, and the developmental objectives while balancing safety, enjoyment, challenge, and progression.

The defining challenge of Lead 4 is not introducing greater complexity.

The defining challenge is consistently exercising sound judgment when no external boundaries dictate the correct decision.

## Core Development Goal

Demonstrate the ability to independently exercise sound judgment while planning, organizing, leading, and evaluating movement jumps of any appropriate complexity.

## Responsibility

Exercise sound judgment while stewarding the entire movement jump experience.

## Prerequisites

Required

- Lead 3 Certification

## Privilege

A Lead 4 flyer may independently determine the appropriate structure, complexity, and execution of movement jumps based upon participant competency, environmental conditions, and operational considerations.

## What This Level Unlocks

- Independent Leadership


## Philosophy

Leadership ultimately becomes a matter of judgment.

The progression system intentionally provides operational boundaries throughout the Leadership Tier to help leaders develop sound decision-making.

Lead 4 represents the point at which those boundaries have been internalized.

A successful Lead 4 flyer no longer depends upon the progression system to determine what is appropriate.

Instead, they consistently demonstrate the ability to establish appropriate boundaries for themselves and for those they lead.


### Demonstrated Discernment

A Lead 4 flyer demonstrates the ability to consistently distinguish between what is possible and what is appropriate.

Discernment requires balancing numerous competing considerations, including participant competency, experience, environmental conditions, operational risk, developmental objectives, and overall jump quality.

The correct decision is not always the most exciting, the most difficult, or the most technically impressive.

The correct decision is the one most likely to produce a safe, successful, enjoyable, and developmentally valuable movement jump.


### Stewardship

A successful Lead 4 flyer understands that leadership is ultimately an act of stewardship.

The objective is not to showcase personal ability.

The objective is to create an environment where participants are safe, appropriately challenged, enjoy the experience, and continue progressing.

Stewardship requires accepting responsibility not only for the execution of the jump, but for the overall experience of every participant.


### Experimentation and Innovation

A Lead 4 flyer recognizes that meaningful progression within movement flight requires experimentation, creativity, and innovation.

Experimentation should be intentional, appropriately planned, and carefully managed.

A successful Lead 4 flyer understands when experimentation creates valuable learning opportunities and when it introduces unnecessary risk.

Innovation is encouraged.

Recklessness is not.

# Part IV: EE Tier

## Purpose

The EE Tier exists to protect the integrity of the Movement Progression Program and the safety of its participants.

Movement flight introduces risks that are often invisible to inexperienced jumpers.

Evaluators and Examiners serve as the program’s guardrails.

The purpose of the EE Tier is not to accelerate progression.

The purpose of the EE Tier is to ensure progression occurs safely, consistently, and at the appropriate pace for each individual.

# Evaluator

## Status

Operationally Complete

## Intent Statement

The Evaluator level represents a commitment to the development of other flyers and the protection of the standards that guide their progression.

Evaluators recognize that movement flight introduces risks that are often invisible to less experienced participants.

An Evaluator serves as both a guide and a guardrail.

The Evaluator’s role is not to accelerate progression, create barriers, reward effort, reward ambition, or reward participation.

The Evaluator’s responsibility is to determine whether a candidate has demonstrated the competency required to safely exercise the privileges associated with a certification.

When an Evaluator signs off a certification, they are making a professional endorsement.

I am willing to personally stand behind the statement that this individual can safely exercise the privileges associated with this certification.

## Responsibility

Develop people while protecting standards.

Neither responsibility may be neglected.

An Evaluator who protects standards but fails to develop people becomes a gatekeeper.

An Evaluator who develops people but fails to protect standards becomes a cheerleader.

Effective Evaluators do both.

The distinction between Lead 4 and Evaluator is not primarily:

- Flying skill
- Technical knowledge
- Experience
- Jump numbers

A Lead 4 may possess equivalent technical capability.

The distinguishing characteristic is acceptance of responsibility for:

- The progression of other people
- The development of other people
- The stewardship of standards

Leaders primarily manage movement jumps.

Evaluators primarily develop people while protecting standards.

### Observation Skills

Purpose:

Observe accurately.

Observation allows the Evaluator to:

- Identify risk
- Assess competency
- Guide development

Accurate observation protects both standards and people.

Primary Failure:

Failing to recognize dangerous behavior that places the candidate or others at risk.

#### EVAL-OBJ-001

Maintain objectivity by evaluating evidence independently of reputation, relationships, popularity, experience, or personal preference.

##### Student Explanation

Objectivity protects fairness and consistency.

Evaluators should strive to reach the same conclusion regardless of who the candidate is.

The question is not:

Who is this person?

The question is:

What does the evidence demonstrate?

##### Evaluator Guidance

A useful self-check is:

If I didn’t know who this person was, what conclusion would I reach from the evidence alone?

The Examiner should assess whether the candidate reaches the same conclusions when candidate identity is masked, such as in blind video review, as when it is known.

#### EVAL-OBS-001

Accurately identify relevant behaviors, decisions, conditions, and outcomes during direct observation or video review.

##### Student Explanation

Good evaluation begins with accurate observation.

Observation requires more than watching.

It requires recognizing what matters.

Evidence arrives through different mediums, and each has blind spots: direct observation captures context video misses, and video captures detail live observation misses.

The Evaluator must extract accurate observations from whichever medium the evidence arrives in.

##### Evaluator Guidance

A primary failure mode is missing critical information that should influence the evaluation decision.

The Examiner should assess accurate observation across both live jumps and video review, comparing the candidate's observations against their own review of the same evidence.

#### EVAL-OBS-002

Identify behaviors, decisions, and conditions that create unnecessary risk for the candidate or others.

##### Student Explanation

Risk identification is the safety-critical core of observation.

A candidate may fly well while making decisions that create risk they cannot yet see.

The Evaluator is responsible for recognizing dangerous behavior even when the jump outcome was successful.

##### Evaluator Guidance

The candidate should demonstrate recognition of risk-creating behavior in observed or reviewed jumps, including risks that did not result in a bad outcome.

The Examiner should treat a missed dangerous behavior as the most serious observation failure.

#### EVAL-OBS-004

Distinguish relevant observations from irrelevant observations.

##### Student Explanation

Not everything visible matters.

Style differences, harmless variations, and personal preferences are noise.

Observations matter when they bear on the published criteria or on safety.

Evaluating noise produces inconsistent outcomes and unfair evaluations.

##### Evaluator Guidance

The candidate should demonstrate filtering: observations cited in their evaluations should trace to published criteria or safety.

The Examiner should probe whether the candidate can articulate why each cited observation matters.

### Competency Recognition Skills

Purpose:

Recognize demonstrated competency accurately and consistently.

Core principle: Evaluators certify competency, not perfection. Evaluators certify demonstrated competency, not future potential.

Primary Failures:

- Confusing competency with perfection
- Certifying future potential
- Certifying reputation
- Certifying isolated success

#### EVAL-CMP-001

Recognize competency rather than perfection.

Distinguish minor imperfections from meaningful deficiencies.

##### Student Explanation

No candidate flies a perfect jump.

Competency means the candidate can safely and reliably exercise the privileges of the certification, not that they perform flawlessly.

Holding candidates to perfection is a standards violation in the other direction.

##### Evaluator Guidance

The candidate should demonstrate Ready decisions in the presence of minor imperfections and articulate why those imperfections do not affect safe privilege exercise.

The Examiner should assess whether the candidate's bar sits at the published standard, neither above nor below it.

Duplication Audit: 🟢 New


#### EVAL-CMP-002

Recognize skill ownership versus isolated success.

Distinguish between:

- A candidate who owns the skill
- A candidate who performed successfully once

##### Student Explanation

One successful demonstration proves the candidate can perform the skill once.

Ownership means the skill holds up across jumps, conditions, and pressure.

Certification is a statement about ownership, so the evidence must show repetition, not luck.

##### Evaluator Guidance

The candidate should demonstrate the ability to articulate what evidence separates ownership from a good day, and to withhold certification when only isolated success has been shown.

The Examiner should assess whether the candidate's evidence threshold reflects the Competency Framework's ownership definition.

Duplication Audit: 🟢 New


#### EVAL-CMP-003

Evaluate demonstrated competency rather than assumed competency.

Base decisions on observable evidence rather than:

- Reputation
- Experience
- Jump numbers
- Expectations
- Potential

##### Student Explanation

Reputation, experience, and jump numbers are not evidence.

A candidate with two thousand jumps must demonstrate the same competencies as a candidate with two hundred.

Assumption is how unqualified candidates get certified and how standards erode.

##### Evaluator Guidance

The candidate should demonstrate identical evidence requirements regardless of who the candidate is.

The Examiner should specifically test this with high-reputation or high-experience candidates, where assumption pressure is strongest.

Duplication Audit: 🟢 New


### Standards Application Skills

Purpose:

Apply published standards consistently, objectively, and professionally.

Primary Failures:

- Evaluating reputation instead of evidence
- Creating personal standards
- Lowering standards for preferred candidates
- Raising standards for disfavored candidates
- Allowing evaluator drift

#### EVAL-STD-001

Apply published standards consistently and independently of personal preference, convenience, or external pressure.

##### Student Explanation

Standards only remain meaningful if they are applied consistently.

##### Evaluator Guidance

A useful self-check is:

Am I applying the published standard, or my personal version of the standard?

Evaluators help candidates reach the standard rather than moving the standard to reach the candidate.

The Examiner should assess consistency of the candidate's standards application across candidates, conditions, and social pressure.

Duplication Audit: 🟢 New


#### EVAL-STD-002

Compare observable evidence directly against published evaluation criteria.

##### Student Explanation

Evaluation is a comparison between what was observed and what the standard requires, and nothing else.

Working from memory of the standard, or from a general impression of the candidate, replaces the published standard with a personal one.

##### Evaluator Guidance

The candidate should demonstrate evaluations that reference specific published criteria, not general impressions.

The Examiner should assess whether the candidate can point to the criterion behind each element of their decision.

Duplication Audit: 🟢 New


#### EVAL-STD-003

Apply the same standard to all candidates.

##### Student Explanation

A standard applied unevenly is not a standard.

Friends, strangers, skilled flyers, and struggling ones all receive the same criteria, the same evidence requirements, and the same outcomes for the same performance.

##### Evaluator Guidance

The candidate should demonstrate consistency across candidates over multiple evaluations.

The Examiner should look for the two failure directions: lowered standards for preferred candidates and raised standards for disfavored ones.

Duplication Audit: 🟢 New


#### EVAL-STD-004

Distinguish deficiencies from minor imperfections.

##### Student Explanation

A deficiency affects the candidate's ability to safely exercise the certification's privileges.

An imperfection does not.

Treating imperfections as deficiencies blocks qualified candidates; treating deficiencies as imperfections certifies unqualified ones.

The distinction is the standard itself.

##### Evaluator Guidance

The candidate should demonstrate correct classification of observed issues against the privilege-relevance test.

The Examiner should present or discuss borderline cases and assess the candidate's reasoning, not just their conclusion.

Duplication Audit: 🟢 New


#### EVAL-STD-005

Avoid introducing requirements that do not exist within the published standard.

##### Student Explanation

Evaluators do not get to add pet requirements: a preferred technique, extra demonstrations, a personal style.

Every requirement a candidate must meet is written down.

If it is not written down, it is not required.

##### Evaluator Guidance

The candidate should demonstrate evaluations free of unpublished requirements.

The Examiner should ask the candidate to source each requirement they applied; anything without a citation to the published standard is a violation of this skill.

Duplication Audit: 🟢 New


#### EVAL-STD-006

Avoid ignoring requirements that do exist within the published standard.

##### Student Explanation

The mirror failure: skipping requirements out of sympathy, schedule pressure, or the sense that a candidate is close enough.

Every published requirement exists for a documented reason.

An Evaluator who waives requirements is silently rewriting the standard.

##### Evaluator Guidance

The candidate should demonstrate complete coverage of published criteria in their evaluations.

The Examiner should audit evaluations for skipped or soft-graded requirements, especially under time or social pressure.

Duplication Audit: 🟢 New


### Sign-Off Decisions Skills

Purpose:

Make responsible certification decisions based upon demonstrated competency and observable evidence.

Primary Failures:

- Certifying potential
- Certifying reputation
- Certifying effort
- Certifying insufficient evidence
- Avoiding difficult decisions

#### EVAL-JDG-001

Exercise sound judgment by accurately distinguishing between imperfections, deficiencies, competency gaps, and meaningful risks.

##### Student Explanation

Not every mistake is a failure.

Not every imperfection is a deficiency.

Not every deficiency creates unacceptable risk.

##### Evaluator Guidance

A useful question is:

Does this issue meaningfully affect the candidate’s ability to safely exercise the privileges associated with this certification?

The Examiner should present borderline cases and assess whether the candidate's classifications trace to privilege-relevant risk rather than personal strictness or leniency.

#### EVAL-STW-001

Protect the integrity of the progression system by preserving standards, consistency, and institutional knowledge.

##### Student Explanation

Evaluators are responsible for more than individual certification decisions.

##### Evaluator Guidance

A useful self-check is:

If every Evaluator made this decision the way I am making it now, what would happen to the standard over time?

The Examiner should assess whether the candidate's decisions would preserve the standard if universalized across all evaluators.

Duplication Audit: 🟢 New


#### EVAL-SGN-001

Determine whether available evidence is sufficient to support certification.

##### Student Explanation

Before asking whether the candidate is competent, ask whether the evidence can answer that question.

Thin evidence (too few jumps, poor video, unobserved criteria) cannot support certification no matter how promising the candidate looks.

The correct response to insufficient evidence is gathering more, not guessing.

##### Evaluator Guidance

The candidate should demonstrate explicit sufficiency judgments before outcome decisions.

The Examiner should assess whether the candidate can articulate what evidence is missing and how to obtain it.

Duplication Audit: 🟢 New


#### EVAL-SGN-002

Issue Ready outcomes only when competency has been clearly demonstrated.

##### Student Explanation

Ready is a professional endorsement with the Evaluator's name on it.

It means the evidence clearly demonstrates the candidate can safely exercise the certification's privileges.

Probably ready is Not Yet.

##### Evaluator Guidance

The candidate should demonstrate Ready decisions that another Evaluator reviewing the same evidence would reach independently.

The Examiner should review a sample of the candidate's Ready decisions against the underlying evidence.

Duplication Audit: 🟢 New


#### EVAL-SGN-003

Issue Not Yet outcomes when competency has not yet been clearly demonstrated.

##### Student Explanation

Not Yet is not a punishment and not a verdict on the person.

It is an accurate statement that the evidence does not yet demonstrate competency, paired with a path forward.

Avoiding Not Yet decisions to spare feelings certifies risk into the sky.

##### Evaluator Guidance

The candidate should demonstrate willingness to issue Not Yet outcomes, including to friends, skilled flyers, and persistent candidates.

The Examiner should weigh how the candidate delivers the outcome: accurately, constructively, and without apology for the standard.

Duplication Audit: 🟢 New


#### EVAL-SGN-004

Separate demonstrated competency from future potential.

##### Student Explanation

They will get there is a prediction, not evidence.

Certification answers what the candidate can do today.

Potential is developed through coaching; it is never certified.

##### Evaluator Guidance

The candidate should demonstrate decisions grounded exclusively in demonstrated performance.

The Examiner should probe cases where the candidate believed in a flyer's trajectory and assess whether that belief leaked into the outcome.

#### EVAL-SGN-005

Accept responsibility for certification decisions.

##### Student Explanation

Every sign-off carries the Evaluator's personal endorsement: I stand behind this decision.

That includes owning the outcome when a certified flyer later demonstrates the certification was premature, and correcting course rather than deflecting.

##### Evaluator Guidance

The candidate should demonstrate ownership language and behavior around their decisions, past and hypothetical.

The Examiner should assess whether the candidate treats sign-offs as endorsements they carry or paperwork they process.

A candidate who understands why something is a deficiency can fix it.

A candidate who only knows they failed cannot.

##### Evaluator Guidance

The candidate should demonstrate explanations that link deficiency, consequence, and correction.

Identify developmental priorities.

Candidates cannot fix everything at once.

The Evaluator identifies which deficiency matters most, usually the one affecting safety or blocking the most downstream progress, and directs effort there first.

A list of ten problems is less useful than the right one.

The candidate should demonstrate prioritization with safety-relevant deficiencies first.

The Examiner should assess whether the candidate can defend the ordering, not just produce it.

Communicate expectations clearly.

Candidates should never be surprised by an evaluation.

Before the jump, they should know what will be evaluated and what the standard requires.

Ambiguity about expectations produces Not Yet outcomes that teach nothing.

The candidate should demonstrate pre-evaluation communication that leaves candidates able to state what is expected of them.

Support growth without lowering standards.

The developmental move is never to soften the standard; it is to strengthen the path to it.

Evaluators help candidates reach the standard rather than moving the standard to reach the candidate.

The candidate should demonstrate encouragement and investment in struggling candidates alongside unmoved standards.

The Examiner should treat any instance of standard-softening framed as kindness as a failure of this skill, not a style difference.

# Examiner

## Status

Operationally Complete

## Development Theme

Institutional Stewardship

An Examiner stewards the progression system itself: the evaluators who apply its standards, the consistency of that application, and the integrity of the standards over time.

## Intent Statement

The Examiner level represents acceptance of responsibility for the progression system as a whole.

An Examiner develops evaluators, certifies evaluators, interprets standards, resolves standards disputes, and participates in the stewardship and evolution of the program.

Where an Evaluator protects standards one candidate at a time, an Examiner protects the standards themselves.

When an Examiner certifies an Evaluator, they are making a professional endorsement.

I am willing to personally stand behind the statement that this individual will develop people and protect standards with the objectivity, judgment, and consistency this program requires.

The Examiner level is not a reward for seniority, reputation, or contribution.

It is the acceptance of responsibility for everything the progression system exists to protect.

## Core Development Goal

Demonstrate the ability to develop and certify evaluators, apply and interpret standards consistently, resolve disputes objectively, and preserve the integrity of the progression system over time.

## Responsibility

Develop evaluators and protect standards.

Neither responsibility may be neglected.

An Examiner who protects standards but fails to develop evaluators starves the program of the people who sustain it.

An Examiner who develops evaluators but fails to protect standards certifies the erosion of the system itself.

## Prerequisites

Required

- Evaluator Certification
- Selection for Examiner development

## Selection

Selection answers the question:

Should this individual begin Examiner development?

Selection is not certification.

Selection grants opportunity.

Certification grants authority.

Examiner candidates are selected by existing Examiners.

Common indicators include:

- Demonstrated objectivity as an Evaluator
- Consistency of standards application
- Sound resolution of difficult sign-off decisions
- Respect earned through stewardship rather than status
- Genuine investment in the development of other evaluators

### Founding Examiners

The initial Examiners are seeded by appointment of the program owner.

This appointment mechanism exists solely to establish the program.

Once founding Examiners are certified, all subsequent Examiner selection and certification occurs through the processes defined in this section, and the founding appointment authority is retired.

## Authority

An Examiner may certify evaluators, interpret standards, resolve standards disputes, and participate in the stewardship and evolution of the progression system.

Interpretation is not modification.

An Examiner clarifies what a standard means and how it applies.

Changing what a standard requires occurs only through the specification change process defined in Part V.

# Part V: Terminology, Governance, and Change Control

## Purpose

The purpose of this section is to:

- Reduce ambiguity.

# Terminology

## Advancement

Advancement is the successful progression from one certification level to another.

Advancement represents acceptance of additional responsibility.

Advancement is not the accumulation of privileges alone.

## Authority

Authority is permission granted by the progression system to exercise specific responsibilities or privileges.

Authority must be explicitly granted.

Authority is never assumed.

Authority is never implied solely by:

- Experience
- Jump numbers
- Reputation
- Technical skill

## Candidate

A candidate is an individual currently being evaluated for certification.

## Certification

Certification is formal recognition that a candidate has demonstrated the competency required to safely exercise the privileges associated with a level.

Certification is granted through sign-off by authorized evaluators.

## Competency

Competency is the demonstrated ability to perform a task:

- Safely
- Correctly
- Consistently
- With appropriate judgment and control

Competency is the primary advancement standard throughout the program.

## Completion

Completion means a task was performed.

Completion alone does not establish competency.

## Development

Development is the process through which an individual increases competency, judgment, responsibility, and capability.

The progression system exists to support development.

Certification exists to recognize development.

## Evaluator

An Evaluator is an individual authorized to certify competency and support participant development while protecting standards.

## Evidence

Evidence is observable information used to support certification decisions.

Evidence may include:

- Direct observation
- Video review
- Structured discussion
- Scenario evaluation

## Examiner

An Examiner is an individual responsible for developing evaluators and protecting standards at the program level.

## Intent Statement

An Intent Statement explains:

- Why a level exists.
- What developmental stage it represents.
- What transformation is expected.

Intent Statements communicate developmental purpose.

## Level

A level is a defined stage within the progression system.

Examples include:

- Solo
- Group
- Lead 1
- Evaluator

Level and Certification may be used interchangeably when context permits.

## Not Yet

Not Yet is an evaluation outcome indicating that competency has not yet been demonstrated.

Not Yet is developmental.

Not Yet is not punitive.

Not Yet is not rejection.

## Ownership

Ownership is the stage beyond competency.

Ownership exists when a behavior appears:

- Comfortable
- Consistent
- Intentional
- Normalized

Ownership indicates deep integration of the competency.

## Privilege

A privilege is an operational capability authorized after certification.

Privileges are earned through demonstrated readiness.

## Requirement

A requirement is an observable competency, knowledge area, or responsibility that must be demonstrated for certification.

## Restriction

A restriction defines an operational boundary that remains in place after certification.

Restrictions communicate what remains unauthorized.

## Responsibility

Responsibility is the primary obligation accepted at a given level.

Responsibility is the organizing principle of progression.

## Sign-Off

A sign-off is a professional endorsement by an authorized evaluator.

A sign-off represents certification of demonstrated competency.

A sign-off is not an administrative action.

## Skill

A skill is an observable competency that can be demonstrated and evaluated.

## Standard

A standard is an accepted expectation used to guide evaluation and certification.

Standards exist to protect people and preserve consistency.

## Stewardship

Stewardship is the responsibility to preserve and improve standards, knowledge, and participant development for future generations.

Stewardship is a foundational concept within the EE Tier.

## Sticker

Sticker is the traditional operational term used within the movement community to describe certifications.

Within this specification:

Sticker and Certification are considered equivalent unless otherwise specified.

# Governance Principles

## GP-001: Explicit Change

Standards should change intentionally.

Accidental change is a form of drift.

All significant modifications should be documented.

## GP-002: Rationale Preservation

Changes should preserve the rationale behind decisions.

When a standard changes, the reason for the change should be documented.

## GP-003: Traceability

Future maintainers should be able to understand:

- What changed
- Why it changed
- When it changed

## GP-004: Consistency Over Convenience

Consistency should generally take precedence over short-term convenience.

Exceptions should be deliberate and documented.

## GP-005: Evolution Is Expected

The progression system is expected to evolve.

The objective is not to prevent change.

The objective is to ensure change occurs intentionally.

# Design Decision Lifecycle

Every Design Decision should exist in one of the following states.

## Draft

Under discussion.

Not yet accepted.

## Accepted

Approved and incorporated into the specification.

## Accepted (Provisional)

Approved but subject to future review.

Used when additional experience is needed before permanent adoption.

## Superseded

Replaced by a newer decision.

Retained for historical context.

## Rejected

Considered and intentionally not adopted.

Retained to prevent future rediscovery.

# Design Decision Management

When creating a Design Decision, the following fields should be documented:

- Decision ID
- Title
- Status
- Date
- Rationale
- Implications

Optional fields:

- Alternatives Considered
- Risks
- Future Review Notes

# Specification Change Process

Major changes should generally follow the sequence:

- Identify issue or opportunity.
- Discuss alternatives.
- Create or modify Design Decision.
- Evaluate implications.
- Update specification.
- Update derivative materials.

The specification remains the master source of truth.

Derivative materials should not independently redefine standards.

# Change Review Safeguards

The following safeguards apply to all future revisions.

## Constitutional Precedence

When conflicts arise between implementation details and constitutional principles, the constitutional principles take precedence.

Every proposed change should first be evaluated against Part I. A change that violates a constitutional principle should not be accepted, regardless of how the wording reads.

Constitutional amendments require extraordinary justification.

## Development Theme Protection

Development Themes are structural, not descriptive. Each theme explains why its level exists and justifies that level's restrictions.

Development Themes require architectural review before modification.

## Privilege Change Review

Privileges are the primary regression surface of the specification.

Every proposed privilege change requires review of the associated:

- Responsibility
- Evaluation Criteria
- Restrictions
- Advancement Path

A privilege change without a corresponding responsibility change should be treated as a probable error.

## Restriction Removal Review

Every restriction in this specification serves a developmental objective.

Before removing a restriction, identify the developmental objective that would disappear with it. If no objective can be identified, investigate before removing; the objective may be implicit rather than absent.

## Competency Identifier Freeze

Competency and skill identifiers (for example, LEAD3-PLN-001) are frozen as of v0.10.

Wording may be edited. Identifiers may not be renumbered or reorganized. New competencies receive new identifiers.

Identifiers may be retired through documented consolidation when requirements are found to duplicate each other. Retired identifiers are never reassigned.

## Appendix Growth Principle

The core specification should remain stable. The appendices are the designated location for growth.

Example dives, evaluation examples, risk examples, and case studies belong in appendices rather than inside progression levels.

# Versioning Philosophy

Version numbers represent specification maturity.

They do not imply program completion.

Examples:

### 0.x

Foundational development.

Significant portions of the program remain under construction.

### 1.x

Complete operational baseline.

Major structural changes become less common.

### 2.x+

Major architectural evolution.

Substantial changes to program structure or philosophy.

# Appendix A: Responsibility Ladder

## Purpose

The Responsibility Ladder is the primary organizing model for progression.

Every level should represent acceptance of additional responsibility.

Skills exist as evidence of readiness for that responsibility.

| Level | Primary Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| Solo | Manage yourself |
| Group | Manage your position within a group |
| Lead 1 | Manage one other flyer |
| Lead 2 | Manage a formation |
| Lead 3 | Introduce and manage complexity appropriately |
| Lead 4 | Exercise sound judgment while stewarding the entire movement jump experience |
| Evaluator | Develop people while protecting standards |
| Examiner | Develop evaluators and protect standards |

## Interpretation

Progression should generally be evaluated through the question:

What new responsibility is being accepted at this level?

If no meaningful increase in responsibility exists, a new level may not be justified.

# Appendix B: Responsibility Transition Model

## Purpose

The Responsibility Transition Model describes the major developmental transitions within the progression system.

| Transition | Responsibility Accepted |
| --- | --- |
| Non-Solo → Solo | Safe personal movement operations |
| Solo → Group | Safe participation within a movement group |
| Group → Leader Tier | Safety and management of other flyers |
| Leader Tier → EE Tier | Development of people and protection of standards |

## Interpretation

These transitions represent major shifts in responsibility.

They should guide future level design and revision.

# Appendix C: Authority Matrix

## Purpose

The Authority Matrix summarizes authority throughout the program.

Authority must always be explicit.

| Role | Authority |
| --- | --- |
| Flyer | Participate in movement jumps within the privileges granted by their current certification level |
| Leader | Plan and manage movement jumps within the privileges granted by their current certification level |
| Evaluator | Certify demonstrated competency; authorize progression within the limits defined by the program |
| Examiner | Certify evaluators; interpret standards; resolve standards disputes; participate in stewardship and evolution of the progression system |

Authority within each role is further limited by the privileges and restrictions of the individual's certification level, as defined in Parts III and IV.

## Notes

Technical skill does not automatically grant authority.

Authority must be intentionally granted.
